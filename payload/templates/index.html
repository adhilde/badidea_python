Why Recycle E-Waste Vs Landfill?

Electronics waste, commonly known as e-scrap or e-waste, is the trash we generate from surplus, broken, and obsolete electronic devices. Electronics contains various toxic and hazardous chemicals and materials that are released into the environment if we do not dispose of them properly. E-waste or electronics recycling is the process of recovering material from old devices to use in new products.

The electronic devices that surround you on a daily basis are built with many components that contain precious metals such as gold, silver, copper, and palladium. From cell phones to modems and computers, old electronics are finding a new lease on life in the electronics recycling marketplace.

Some states such as California, New York, Utah, Michigan, Texas, and others, have passed e-waste laws and legislation. The laws passed at the state level often include policies that define where take back or recycling programs are required. Other commonly defined policies include a list of e-waste restrictions and fines imposed for units found in landfill or abandoned.
